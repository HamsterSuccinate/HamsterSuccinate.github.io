My Suicide Prevention Day

Posted on 2020-11-04 Edited on 2020-11-19 In comedy

Eight in the morning. I have made my five-hour sex work at Logan circle another good one and retired to my cozy apartment. I am so proud of myself. Numbly inured to the squirming I now pay way more attention to insect chirping than the glorified spigot or the fleshy cylinder attached to it. Usually after work I chill with whisky and chamber music. Not today, World Suicide Prevention Day.

Marginalized and stigmatized , I desperately want to help. I am aching to prove my superiority and righteousness over someone. Who is more marginalized and stigmatized than a non-suicidal sex worker ? A suicidal one.

In my profession everyone is depressed and traumatized. When Spring agitates our nerves many of us drop dead. Anyhow our(?) society must prevent suicide in autumn when people are least likely to commit suicide. This is how we(?) can prove the effectiveness of our(?) intervention.

The Suicide-Prevention-Day hashtag is trending on Twitter. Seeing so many people justify their obsessive attachment to a rotting body I feel I belong. The tweets, especially official tweets, are so informative and inspiring that I take notes :

  • “Suicide is preventable”. I feel so empowered. Better if any tweets explains why it is preventable. Or suicide is only preventable in the sense that non-suicidal people must not feel powerless.
  • “Warning signs”. There have to be warning signs otherwise suicide cannot be prevented. But how warning are the warning signs? What is the probability of false positive and false negative?
  • “Mental illnesses”. Medical charlatans exploit the fear of death. Mentally healthy people live and die as medical authorities ordain saving accidents and natural disasters. Hence dying on one’s own term has to be medicated. Once upon a time suicide was a “crime”. Law enforcement dudes can grieve their lost glory.
  • “Depression”. Some manage to depress themselves below the snare woven by fibers of incentives. They shirk their “responsibilities”. They stop “functioning”. They are even indifferent to physical pain. Their Zen abstinence makes us appear needy, spoiled and avaricious. Their freedom make us jealous. Unacceptable! They must be found out and stigmatized.
  • “You are not alone”. Writ large. It eludes me why it is supposed to make someone less suicidal. Don’t people kill themselves because stressors won’t leave them alone? Most people in my noble profession kill themselves after being stalked by clients. For Pete’s sake, please tell me “you are alone” so I can keep calm and carry on.
  • “You need help”. Exactly the suicidal need “help” to not upset the non-suicidal.
  • “Report to authorities” . Shovel them behind a door. Get them out of my life-loving sight. Uh, what if the suicidal loved one gets hurt by police brutality? That’s not MY problem. All the of us privileged to be non-suicidal would agree that It is better to be safe than sorry. As long as I am not sorry it is irrelevant whether the suicidal loved one is damaged or not. Amulet of absolution : I want to help.

Yawn, I am getting sleepy. I officially announce that I have prevented suicide enough on suicide prevention day. Good day to myself. Wish me a good dream about stalking clients. After all they are the true memories of melancholy whores.

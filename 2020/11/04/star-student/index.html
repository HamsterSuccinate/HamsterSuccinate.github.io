Monologue of a Rising Senior

Posted on 2020-11-04 Edited on 2020-12-24 In comedy

I am a rising senior of a top high school in the wealthiest state of the — um, I am not sure anymore, great-ever-so-does-not-need-to-be-great-again or great-once-not-so-great-now-but-will-be-great-again United States. Guess which state ? Tip . George Carlin : I knew a transsexual guy whose only ambition is to eat , drink and be Mary. Yes, Maryland. O Mary—land, not your land but maybe my land. I know many non-LGBTQ people who also long to be Mary to be in Maryland.

Let me inspire you with my rise. Sorry (not really) if it makes those less accomplished look leveling or even falling. I cannot help just as Covid19 cannot help dwarfing plain vanilla flus. I would still be rising if I could do it all over again. I vicariously appreciate their not-one-of-the-greatest-ness. Thanks to them I get to be one of the greatest. Can we just erase “one of “ ?

First off, I am the president of the thespian club. I cannot just be one of the team. There is no I in team but there is definitely an I in president. Anyway I do put my heart and soul into drama. I need to be really good at “getting into character”, that is , the paradoxical art of artificially generating genuine emotions as needed. Those who superficially arrange their face or tones while saying the right thing are quickly red-flagged as manipulators. I am the pro. Everyone is impressed with my integrity. People consider it a favor to them if they can do something for me. It goes without saying that my letters of recommendation are going to be fabulous.

Second, I am the president of a swimming team. Not the swimming team. The top-rated high school has multiple teams so that many can write about not only athletic but leadership talents in college application. Runners used to be thought of as being hardworking and strong-willed. Perfect characters to be exploited by future employers. Now that Hale Ross and Madison Holleran strong-willed themselves out of exploitation once and for all, their reputation has been eclipsed a bit. Swimming is in vogue.

Third, I am an ambassador of Women in STEM. Sounds wonderfully progressive, doesn’t it ? As an ambassador my goal is to create more opportunities for girls to pursue STEM at both the high school and middle school level. See. I have such a noble goal and I will make a difference. Opportunities are not to be until I let there be. Hapless young girls are not in the position to create opportunities for themselves. Viola, let there be opportunities. Just a simple feeling of goddess.

Fourth, I am a member of science and Spanish honor societies. I won the first grand prize in science fair all by myself. My mom just happened to be a career adviser of the engineering department of a university. And I am fluent in Spanish not because mi padre is a native speaker and mi madre has a master’s degree in Spanish. I live as white in daily life. But swiftly and quietly I will check the Latino and Hispanic box in college application to ride the affirmative action pony. I love THIS diversity ! Somehow Asian Americans do not seem to share my love but whatever. May diversity be diversified and inclusion included. Once again I am so sorry (not really) that I have to be in an honor society. If I am not, those who are not in an honor society will end up in a dishonor society.

Last but not least, I am interning at the National Cancer Institute to study signal pathways. Ever noticed how parents persuade childless people into reproduction? “Your kids may cure CANCER” . It makes perfect sense that parents cannot cure cancer but nonetheless are eager to bring kids into this cancer-ridden world. By the way I got the internship because of my impressive credentials and great thespian personality. It is pure coincidence that mi madre now lodges herself in the public health department of the university. Hang in there good old CANCER, the society will grant me money and authority all because they fear you.

So much for showcasing my rise. Now I am back to rising. Unlike the poor Icarus I will rise from sky to elitist sky.

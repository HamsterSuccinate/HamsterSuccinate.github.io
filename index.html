My Suicide Prevention Day

Posted on 2020-11-04 In satire

Eight in the morning. I have made my five-hour sex work at Logan circle another good one and retired to my cozy apartment. I am so proud of myself. Numbly inured to the squirming I now pay way more attention to insect chirping than the glorified spigot or the fleshy cylinder attached to it. Usually after work I chill with whisky and chamber music. Not today, World Suicide Prevention Day.

Marginalized and stigmatized , I desperately want to help. I am aching to prove my superiority and righteousness over someone. Who is more marginalized and stigmatized than a non-suicidal sex worker ? A suicidal one.

In my profession everyone is depressed and traumatized. When Spring agitates our nerves many of us drop dead. Anyhow our(?) society must prevent suicide in autumn when people are least likely to commit suicide. This is how we(?) can prove the effectiveness of our(?) intervention.

The Suicide-Prevention-Day hashtag is trending on Twitter. Seeing so many people justify their obsessive attachment to a rotting body I feel I belong. The tweets, especially official tweets, are so informative and inspiring that I take notes :

  • “Suicide is preventable”. I feel so empowered. Better if any tweets explains why it is preventable. Or suicide is only preventable in the sense that non-suicidal people must not feel powerless.
  • “Warning signs”. There have to be warning signs otherwise suicide cannot be prevented. But how warning are the warning signs? What is the probability of false positive and false negative?
  • “Mental illnesses”. Medical charlatans exploit the fear of death. Mentally healthy people live and die as medical authorities ordain saving accidents and natural disasters. Hence dying on one’s own term has to be medicated. Once upon a time suicide was a “crime”. Law enforcement dudes can grieve their lost glory.
  • “Depression”. Some manage to depress themselves below the snare woven by fibers of incentives. They shirk their “responsibilities”. They stop “functioning”. They are even indifferent to physical pain. Their Zen abstinence makes us appear needy, spoiled and avaricious. Their freedom make us jealous. Unacceptable! They must be found out and stigmatized.
  • “You are not alone”. Writ large. It eludes me why it is supposed to make someone less suicidal. Don’t people kill themselves because stressors won’t leave them alone? Most people in my noble profession kill themselves after being stalked by clients. For Pete’s sake, please tell me “you are alone” so I can keep calm and carry on.
  • “You need help”. Exactly the suicidal need “help” to not upset the non-suicidal.
  • “Report to authorities” . Shovel them behind a door. Get them out of my life-loving sight. Uh, what if the suicidal loved one gets hurt by police brutality? That’s not MY problem. All the of us privileged to be non-suicidal would agree that It is better to be safe than sorry. As long as I am not sorry it is irrelevant whether the suicidal loved one is damaged or not. Amulet of absolution : I want to help.

Yawn, I am getting sleepy. I officially announce that I have prevented suicide enough on suicide prevention day. Good day to myself. Wish me a good dream about stalking clients. After all they are the true memories of melancholy whores.

Monologue of a Rising Senior

Posted on 2020-11-04 In comedy

I am a rising senior of a top high school in the wealthiest state of the — um, I am not sure anymore, great-ever-so-does-not-need-to-be-great-again or great-once-not-so-great-now-but-will-be-great-again United States. Guess which state ? Tip . George Carlin : I knew a transsexual guy whose only ambition is to eat , drink and be Mary. Yes, Maryland. O Mary—land, not your land but maybe my land. I know many non-LGBTQ people who also long to Mary to be in Maryland.

Let me inspire you with my rise. Sorry (not really) if it makes those less accomplished look leveling or even falling. I cannot help just as Covid19 cannot help dwarfing plain vanilla flus. I would still be rising if I could do it all over again. I vicariously appreciate their not-one-of-the-greatest-ness. Thanks to them I get to be one of the greatest. Can we just erase “one of “ ?

First off, I am the president of the thespian club. I cannot just be one of the team. There is no I in team but there is definitely an I in president. Anyway I do put my heart and soul into drama. I need to be really good at “getting into character”, that is , the paradoxical art of artificially generating genuine emotions as needed. Those who superficially arrange their face or tones while saying the right thing are quickly red-flagged as manipulators. I am the pro. Everyone is impressed with my integrity. People consider it a favor to them if they can do something for me. It goes without saying that my letters of recommendation are going to be fabulous.

Second, I am the president of a swimming team. Not the swimming team. The top-rated high school has multiple teams so that many can write about not only athletic but leadership talents in college application. Runners used to be thought of as being hardworking and strong-willed. Perfect characters to be exploited by future employers. Now that Hale Ross and Madison Holleran strong-willed themselves out of exploitation once and for all, their reputation has been eclipsed a bit. Swimming is in vogue.

Third, I am an ambassador of Women in STEM. Sounds wonderfully progressive, doesn’t it ? As an ambassador my goal is to create more opportunities for girls to pursue STEM at both the high school and middle school level. See. I have such a noble goal and I will make a difference. Opportunities are not to be until I let there be. Hapless young girls are not in the position to create opportunities for themselves.

Fourth, I am a member of science and Spanish honor society. I won the first grand prize in science fair all by myself. My mom just happened to be a career adviser of the engineering department of a university. And I am fluent in Spanish not because mi padre is a native speaker and mi madre has a master’s degree in Spanish. I live as white in daily life. But swiftly and quietly I will check the Latino and Hispanic box in college application to ride the affirmative action pony. I love THIS diversity ! Somehow Asian Americans do not seem to share my love but whatever. May diversity be diversified and inclusion included. Once again I am so sorry (not really) that I have to be in an honor society. If I am not, those who are not in an honor society will end up in a dishonor society.

Last but not least, I am interning at the National Cancer Institute to study signal pathways. Ever noticed how parents persuade childless people into reproduction? “Your kids may cure CANCER” . It makes perfect sense that parents cannot cure cancer but nonetheless are eager to bring kids into this cancer-ridden world. By the way I got the internship because of my impressive credentials and great thespian personality. It is pure coincidence that mi madre now lodges herself in the public health department of the university. Hang in there good old CANCER, the society grants me money and authority all because they fear you.

So much for showcasing my rise. Now I am back to rising. Unlike the poor Icarus I will rise from sky to elitist sky.
